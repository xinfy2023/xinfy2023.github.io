TurboRANSAC: A Fully GPU-Accelerated RANSAC Framework for Robust Estimation

Accepted to IEEE ISCAS 2026 (Co-first author) [PDF]

Robust model estimation plays a central role in geometric computer vision, enabling applications such as camera localization, motion estimation, and 3D reconstruction. Among many techniques, Random Sample Consensus (RANSAC) is widely adopted for its robustness to outliers. However, existing RANSAC variants commonly rely on CPU-based sequential processing, leading to computational inefficiency that limits their suitability for real-time and large-scale scenarios. This challenge is heightened by the rise of learning-based pipelines optimized for GPU acceleration, where CPU-bound RANSAC becomes a bottleneck within predominantly GPU-centric workflows.

TurboRANSAC is a fully GPU-accelerated framework that parallelizes all key stages, including sampling, hypothesis generation, scoring, and refinement, directly on GPU. Unlike methods that optimize isolated components, TurboRANSAC provides a unified and scalable solution applicable to a broad spectrum of geometric estimation problems.

ProbML 2026 Workshop Track (Seoul, South Korea, Jul 05, 2026)

Status: under review
  • Project: Locked Evidence for Shift-Risk Gains on NOAA Climate Regression.
  • Builds a real spatiotemporal-shift benchmark for NOAA climate regression and validates stable OOD risk-ranking gains from E-NGBoost with density-calibrated uncertainty (E+IF).
  • Dataset and tasks: 8 US stations, daily data from 2010 to 2024, and 3 next-day tasks (temperature, wind speed, precipitation), each with over 25k samples.
  • Method: inference-side calibration of evidential epistemic scores using train-only density, without changing the training objective.
  • Evaluation protocol: 6 pre-locked primary hypotheses, one-sided paired Wilcoxon, bootstrap CIs, and Holm correction to avoid post-hoc bias.
  • Results: 6/6 primary hypotheses remain significant after Holm (max adjusted p=0.006836), with gains over strong baselines on temporal/geographic AUROC and extreme-event AURC, Risk@80, and FPR@95.

2DGUT: Distortion-Robust Surface World Representation (3DGUT project)

Ongoing
  • For fisheye and strongly distorted cameras, integrates Unscented Transform (UT) projection and ray-plane surfel primitives into a Gaussian Splatting reconstruction pipeline for better geometric and optimization stability under nonlinear projection.
  • Implements a staged training pipeline (transfer → surface refine → metric recover) and evaluation protocol (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS plus geometric metrics).
  • On Lego fisheye (180°), improves PSNR from 22.48 to 31.65; under matched evaluator, achieves 31.83 vs 28.14 (+3.69 dB) against canonical 2DGS (no-UT).
  • Shows stable gains on multiple ScanNet++ DSLR scenes (up to +1.36 dB on the main scene; all 3 added scenes are positive), with reproducible logs and visualization/ablation analyses completed.

Differentiable one-pass hybrid renderer: unified 3DGS + textured mesh framework (CUDA)

Manuscript in preparation (planned submission to ACM TOG)
  • Developing a CUDA-based one-pass differentiable hybrid rendering framework that unifies 3D Gaussian Splatting and textured meshes.
  • Treats triangles as native primitives and fuses them with Gaussians in the same tile queue via key sorting, enabling end-to-end backpropagation (including texture gradients).
  • Systematically evaluates and optimizes both quality (PSNR) and performance (FPS).

Active view collection + 3D Gaussian Splatting for semantic target reconstruction (Habitat/Replica)

Manuscript in preparation (planned submission to IEEE RA-L)
  • Built an end-to-end system that combines active navigation and 3DGS reconstruction for semantic targets (e.g., chair/bed/toilet).
  • Proposed a navigation-reconstruction decoupled Two-Pass pipeline: a policy generates trajectories under its native observations; then trajectories are replayed with a true fisheye camera model and BVH/Warp ray-tracing depth supervision to export aligned RGB/Depth/Pose/Hit-mask.
  • Designed a coverage-aware evaluation protocol (Coverage@1m, PSNR_all/PSNR_covered, target coverage) to reduce bias and improve reproducibility.
  • Internal experiments (fixed seeds, full test/target views) show a significant improvement in target-region quality under similar target coverage (Target PSNR_covered@1m: ~+9 dB).

Reproducible multi-modal training/evaluation framework for autonomous driving (Bilateral Driving backbone)

Ongoing (planned submission to NeurIPS 2026)
  • Project site: Institute for AI Industry Research, Tsinghua University (AIR).
  • Goal: unify Camera/LiDAR/Radar and distorted-camera rendering into a single reproducible “one-click train + one-click eval” framework, and produce simulatable multi-modal rendering outputs.
  • Reviewer-facing design: avoid a “stitched system” by keeping appearance correction separated from physical modalities while using a unified math rule with modality-specific efficient implementations.
  • Efficiency line: constant-capacity multi-modal fusion via shared representation pools with learned gating/masks (inspired by PackNet), plus exploring Sobolev/Laplacian-style preconditioning for faster and more stable optimization.
